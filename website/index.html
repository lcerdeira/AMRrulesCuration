Overview

This repository is the home of the ESGEM-AMR Working Group, which focuses on curating organism-specific rule sets for interpreting AMR genotypes under the AMRrules framework.

We have partnered with ESGEM, the ESCMID Study Group on Epidemiological Markers, to form an ESGEM-AMR Working Group to curate organism-specific rule sets.

The goal of AMRrules is to develop interpretive standards for AMR genotypes, akin to the interpretive standards developed by EUCAST and CLSI for antimicrobial susceptibility phenotyping.

Key Objectives

What We're Working On

The ESGEM-AMR Working Group develops and validates interpretive rules for the following AMR variant types:

  • Gene presence detection
  • Amino acid substitutions and insertions
  • Nucleotide substitutions and insertions
  • Gene truncation (loss of function)
  • Promoter region mutations
  • Gene copy number changes
  • Mutations in multi-copy genes
  • Low frequency variants

Leadership & Coordinators

The ESGEM-AMR Working Group is led by:

  • Kat Holt (LSHTM) - Convenor
  • Natacha Couto (ESGEM Chair) - Convenor
  • Jane Hawkey (Monash University) - Leading Bioinformatics Development

A call for members was launched in April 2024 and received over 120 applications. The group has now grown to 175+ members organized into 19 organism-focused subgroups.

For a complete list of members by subgroup, please refer to the README.md file.

Partners & Collaborations

EUCAST Partnership

We partner with EUCAST (European Committee on Antimicrobial Susceptibility Testing) to ensure alignment with:

  • EUCAST Subcommittee on WGS and Phenotypic AST
  • Expected Phenotypes
  • Expert Rules
  • Wildtype distributions and ECOFFS
  • Resistance Mechanisms Database
